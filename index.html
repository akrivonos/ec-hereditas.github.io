"Hereditas" is Latin for heritage – the living continuity of practices, narratives, and local knowledge.

Mission

To document, analyze, and compare Europe’s vernacular spiritual traditions with scholarly rigor, open methods, and ethical fieldwork, while amplifying local voices and contexts.

  • Publish accessible research and datasets.
  • Run small, targeted ethnographic expeditions in the Carpathians.
  • Host dialogues that bridge folklore, anthropology, history, and religious studies.

Research Focus

  • Comparative demonology and folk magic (with Hutsul emphasis).
  • Ritual specialists, healing practices, and trance/vision narratives.
  • Place‑based cosmologies: mountains, forests, rivers; shrines and household rites.
  • Oral genres: legends, charms, prayer texts, songs, and ritual speech.

Methods: archival work, textual analysis, field interviews, participant observation, and ethical data stewardship.
